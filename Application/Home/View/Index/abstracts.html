Call for Abstracts

Following the successful ICB2015 Conference in Vancouver, Canada, in August 2015, the ICB2017 Conference will be held in Christchurch, New Zealand. The conference will consist of plenary lectures, keynote speeches, oral presentations and poster sessions. Topics to be covered include bioenergy, bio-based materials and chemicals, integration and integrated assessment of biorefinery technologies and processes, which include but are not limited to:

Feed stocks

  • Energy crops
  • Microalgae
  • Forest and agriculture residues
  • Marine biomass

Pretreatment

  • Biomass drying and sizing
  • Pulping
  • Thermal-mechanical treatment
  • Thermal-chemical treatment
  • Biological treatment

Conversion technologies

  • Combustion
  • Hydrolysis
  • Pyrolysis
  • Gasification
  • Chemical conversion
  • Biochemical conversion and fermentation

Bio-based materials and chemicals

  • Biopolymers
  • Bio-based materials
  • Platform chemicals
  • High value bioproducts

Synthetic biology and platform technology

  • Synthetic biology
  • Extraction, separation and purification technologies
  • Design of bioreactors and photobioreactors
  • Process control engineering

Integrated assessments and evaluation

  • Political evaluation
  • Economic evaluation
  • Environmental impact evaluation

All scholars and experts from the academia, governments and industries are invited to participate in this conference by submitting one full page abstract for the consideration of oral or poster presentations at the conference by the deadline of August 31, 2016. Selected presentations are encouraged to submit a full paper online at by November 1, 2016. The full papers will be assessed and selected papers will be published as special issues in international journals of Biotechnology Advances, Biomass and Bioenergy and energies.

During the conference, a biorefinery industrial workshop will be held in which representatives from commercial companies will share success stories with the audience on bioenergy, biofuels, biomaterials and biochemical.

Instructions for Authors:

  • Abstracts must be written in English not exceeding 400 words and must be submitted online as a Word file (xxx.doc or xxx.docx)
  • Type title (uppercasing the first letter of each word), use Times New Roman 14
  • The presenting author must be underlined, type first name followed by family name
  • Type affiliation underneath the authors: Dept., University/Company, City, Country
  • Add the email of the presenting author underneath the affiliation
  • Use font Times New Roman, 11 characters per inch (font size 11)
  • Use multiple line spacing 1.15
